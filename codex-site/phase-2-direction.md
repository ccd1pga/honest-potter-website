# Phase 2 Direction

## Working Position

The Honest Potter website should become the real public home for an early-stage potter developing a body of work, not a hard-selling ecommerce site.

The tone should feel honest, local, curious, and craft-led. It should make visitors feel they have found a real person making and learning, not a polished brand pretending to be bigger than it is.

## Primary Audience

- People browsing pottery and ceramics online.
- Local visitors, with a gentle Liverpool / Merseyside signal where useful.
- People who might later see work at a stall, local event, or through word of mouth.
- People coming from Kiln Share who need practical kiln-hire information.

The site should be ready for future local moments, such as a homepage banner for a market stall, exhibition, table, open studio, or small batch of available pieces.

## Main Job

The main job is to showcase the work and development of a new potter.

The site should:

- give the gallery a stronger role as the main proof of work
- show progress, experimentation, and skill development
- make it easy to get in touch if someone is interested
- support kiln-hire traffic without making kiln hire the whole identity of the site
- leave room for future selling without pretending the shop is ready now

## What The Site Is Not Yet

- Not primarily a shop.
- Not a full ecommerce experience.
- Not a finished professional pottery brand with a fixed catalogue.
- Not a blog farm or content machine.

It can still have shop-shaped future space, but selling should stay secondary until there are enough pieces, prices, stock states, and fulfilment decisions to support it properly.

## Content Direction

### Gallery

The gallery should remain central. It can be less about perfect captions for now and more about showing range, materials, forms, and development.

### Glaze / Development

A new glaze-development area is worth exploring. The best first version is probably not a formal blog. Better options:

- a visual journal
- a glaze test notebook
- short story-board entries
- recipes being tried, with sample images and notes
- before/after or test-tile grids

This can be modest and practical. It should feel like a studio notebook visitors are allowed to peek into.

### Contact

The contact page is good enough as the main action for now. If people are interested in a piece, a commission, kiln hire, or a future stall, they can get in touch.

### Kiln Hire

Kiln hire is currently mostly supported by Kiln Share redirects. The site should keep a clear kiln-hire page, but it should not overpower the pottery/gallery identity unless traffic or demand changes.

## Homepage Direction

The homepage should become the real front door:

- immediate identity: The Honest Potter
- short explanation: handmade pottery, ceramic experiments, and kiln hire in/near Liverpool
- primary action: view the gallery
- secondary action: contact / kiln hire
- future flexible banner area for market stalls, events, or available pieces
- visual proof from selected work
- a small studio-development section that can lead to glaze notes later

## Navigation Direction

Likely future navigation:

- Home
- Gallery
- Studio Notes or Glaze Journal
- Kiln Hire
- About
- Contact

Shop should either remain a quiet coming-soon page or be removed from primary navigation until there is a real shop plan.

Draft the real site map and homepage structure before changing the live site.
