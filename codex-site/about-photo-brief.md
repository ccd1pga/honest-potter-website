# About Page Photo Brief

No formal portrait is needed. The strongest images for this site are the ones
that show the work, the process, and the studio honestly.

Useful photos to gather:

- A clean hero shot of three to five favourite finished pieces together.
- Close details of rims, handles, carved marks, glaze runs, or texture.
- Hands working with clay, trimming, glazing, loading, or carrying pots.
- A tidy corner of the studio with tools, shelves, clay, or test tiles.
- A kiln-loading or kiln-opening moment, ideally with warm natural light.
- A few simple process shots for the future Studio Notes section.

Avoid for now:

- Formal headshots if they feel uncomfortable.
- Busy room-wide photos where the work gets lost.
- Dark images where the clay or glaze surface is hard to read.
- Highly filtered images that make the colours unreliable.
